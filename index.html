Mathematical Formulation (Logistic Growth Kinetics)

State Function: Cumulative Biomass

$$f(t) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(t - t_0)}}$$

Models cumulative flush weight of Grey Oyster Mushrooms over time $t$ (days). Where $L = 120\text{g}$ is carrying capacity, $k = 0.5\text{ day}^{-1}$ is growth velocity, and $t_0 = 12\text{ days}$ is the peak growth inflection point.

Derivative: Instantaneous Growth Rate

$$f'(t) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t-t_0)}}{\left(1 + e^{-k(t-t_0)}\right)^2}$$

Represents the exact rate of change of biomass accumulation (grams per day) at any instant $t$. It is derived using the Chain Rule and Quotient Rule.

Malaysian Bioscience Case Study Context

In Malaysia's tropical climate, cultivating the Grey Oyster Mushroom (Cendawan Tiram Kelabu) is highly profitable. However, optimization of harvest scheduling is critical for post-harvest food quality.

According to research by MARDI (Agro-Technology Journal, Vol. 12), mushrooms must be harvested at the tail-end of their acceleration phase — precisely when the 2nd derivative is negative ($f''(t) < 0$) and the growth rate $f'(t)$ is robust but beginning to decelerate.

Agro-Tech Secret: If harvested past Day 16, the cap margins split, fiber content increases rapidly, and sporulation drops market value. The derivative calculations allow farm managers to predict optimal crop shelf-life using automated vision sensors.

Understanding the Calculus behind Mushroom Growth

1. The Growth Model

The cumulative biomass of the mushroom is modeled using a Sigmoid (Logistic) function. In our equation:

  • Carrying Capacity ($L$ = 120g): The maximum harvestable weight of a single mushroom flush out of a standard 900g sawdust spawn bag.
  • Inflexion point ($t_0$ = Day 12): The peak growth timing.
  • Growth Coefficient ($k$ = 0.5): Dictates how sharp the rapid-expansion phase is.

2. Step-by-Step Derivation

Let's write our biomass function as: $$f(t) = L \cdot [1 + e^{-k(t-t_0)}]^{-1}$$

Applying the Chain Rule, the derivative is: $$f'(t) = -L \cdot [1 + e^{-k(t-t_0)}]^{-2} \cdot \frac{d}{dt}\left(1 + e^{-k(t-t_0)}\right)$$ $$f'(t) = -L \cdot [1 + e^{-k(t-t_0)}]^{-2} \cdot (-k e^{-k(t-t_0)})$$ $$f'(t) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(t-t_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(t-t_0)})^2}$$

3. Agro-Tech Decisions

By matching crop sensors with these derivative curves, food processors can automate their operations:

  • $f'(t) \approx 0$ (Days 0-7): Inoculation & Spawn Running. Keep incubator rooms dark.
  • $f''(t) > 0$ (Days 8-11): Pinning phase. Initiate high-pressure humidifiers.
  • Peak $f'(t)$ (Day 12): Extreme metabolic activity. Mushroom size doubles in 24 hrs.
  • $f''(t) < 0$ (Days 13-16): Optimal harvesting slot. Perfect crispness and high protein profile.
